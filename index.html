I am a third-year PhD Candidate in Economics at the Faculty of Legal, Economic and Social Sciences in Mohammedia, affiliated with the Laboratory of Economic Performance and Logistics.

My research focuses on macroeconomic policy analysis, with a strong emphasis on bridging theoretical economic frameworks and empirical real-world dynamics. My doctoral work examines the effectiveness of monetary interventions, transmission mechanisms, and central bank responses to economic shocks. More broadly, I explore how central bank independence and policy frameworks shape inflation control and macroeconomic stability, drawing on evidence from both global contexts and the Moroccan economy, including the experience of Bank Al-Maghrib.
